Ancient & Medieval Era

Women in Ancient Medicine

In the Ancient and Medieval eras, women were often excluded from formal medical practice. However, some women did play a role in healing, primarily within their communities. Here are a few notable women from this time:

"I am a woman of science."

Hypatia was a philosopher and mathematician in ancient Alexandria, contributing significantly to science and medicine.

"All the medical knowledge of the world is a reflection of God’s truth."

Hildegard was a Benedictine abbess, composer, and visionary who contributed to both the fields of botany and medicine.

"Women must be treated with care and compassion, as they are the backbone of society."

Trotula, an Italian physician, is credited with writing several texts on women's health in the Middle Ages.

This era relates to the NHD theme of 'Rights and Responsibilities' by showing how women’s roles in medicine were often limited yet crucial for the well-being of their communities.
