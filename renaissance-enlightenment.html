Renaissance & Enlightenment Era

Women in Renaissance Medicine

During the Renaissance and Enlightenment periods, women started to gain more recognition in medicine. Female healers, such as midwives and herbalists, emerged as pioneers in the field.

"I want to understand the true nature of the plants and animals that surround us."

Margaretha Merian was a pioneering scientist known for her studies of insects and plants. She contributed to the medical understanding of the natural world.

"Healing is a form of divine mercy."

Fabiola was a prominent Christian who established a hospital for the poor, providing healthcare services for the less fortunate during the Renaissance.
