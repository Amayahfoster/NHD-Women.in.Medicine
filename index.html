Introduction

Explore the history of women in medicine, from ancient times to the present day. Each era features trailblazing women who helped shape the medical field.

Ancient & Medieval Era (Pre-1500s)

Women were often excluded from formal medical training but still practiced herbal medicine and midwifery.

Renaissance & Enlightenment Era (1500s-1700s)

During this period, women like Elizabeth Blackwell began to challenge gender roles and enter the medical field.

19th Century (1800s)

The 19th century saw more women entering formal medical education and contributing to public health reforms.

Early 20th Century (1900s-1940s)

Women began to gain greater recognition in medicine, with pioneers like Virginia Apgar making groundbreaking contributions.

Modern Era (1950s-Present)

Today, women have become leaders in medical research, healthcare policy, and clinical practice.
