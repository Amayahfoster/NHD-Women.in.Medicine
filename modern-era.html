Modern Era

Women in Modern Medicine

The modern era has seen tremendous advancements in medical research, treatment, and healthcare systems. Women have played crucial roles in these developments, from leading medical research to inspiring social change in the healthcare system.

"Health is a human right, not a privilege!"

Dr. Gro Harlem Brundtland, former Director-General of the World Health Organization, significantly contributed to global health policies and reforms.

"Cancer research should be focused on improving the patient's survival."

Dr. Jane Cooke Wright was an African American physician who developed innovative chemotherapy techniques and contributed significantly to cancer treatment.

"Our job is to save lives, whether it is in our own country or around the world."

Dr. Deborah L. Birx was an American physician and diplomat who served as the U.S. Global AIDS Coordinator and made important contributions to global HIV/AIDS prevention and treatment.

The modern era continues to showcase the empowerment of women in medicine, illustrating how women's rights and responsibilities have transformed in the field over time.
